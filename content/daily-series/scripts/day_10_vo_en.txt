Day 10. I woke up and Twitter had taken my account. No warning. No reason given.

I can't code. 9 years in crypto. AI trades my thousand. Day 10.

The moment it happened, I realized every thread I'd written, every follower, every receipt I'd posted — all of it, held by one platform that didn't call first.

Same day I did something that sounded unrelated — I launched bilingual mode on my own website.

At the time it felt small. The week I had nowhere else to speak, it stopped feeling small.

There's a sentence I'd heard for years and always dismissed as cheesy — don't build your most valuable thing on top of ground you don't own.

That day, that sentence became an invoice.

From Day 10 on — my own site is home base. Every other platform is just distribution. The kind that can be turned off at any time.
