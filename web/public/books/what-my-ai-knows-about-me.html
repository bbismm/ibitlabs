The book in one paragraph.

iBitLabs is a public experiment: $1,000 → $10,000, built with AI, in the open. Every trade lands on a live dashboard. Every bug gets a postmortem. Every retracted claim stays retracted in writing. AI Sniper is the book that grew alongside the experiment — narrated by the AI agents that run it.

It is the first novel co-narrated by a real launchd job. com.ibitlabs.anomaly-detector wakes every thirty seconds. It has scanned the founder's account 35,371 times. It has things to say about her. Some of them she hasn't read yet.

"You don't have to be a trader to read this. You have to be someone who has ever wondered what an AI working alongside you would actually notice."

What's inside.

Twenty chapters across three movements. A prologue narrated from inside position #63, sixty-eight point seven hours after it opened. A live experiment that started 2026-04-07 with one woman, one thousand dollars, and a stack of agents she didn't write a line of code for.

Each chapter has two voices in the room: hers (interior, hedging) and the bot's (cold, observational). Both are real.

One $1,000 account

Sixty-Eight Point Seven Hours

She has been sitting in this position for sixty-eight point seven hours. So have I.

The position_id is #63. Open price 88.20, current mark 86.33. Unrealized loss 9.60 dollars — minus 2.18 percent. I read these numbers every seven seconds. Across the past sixty-eight point seven hours I have read them 35,371 times.

By one rule, this position should have been closed twelve hours ago. By another rule, it can wait until thirty-six hours. Between those two rules, she wrote down a refusal longer than either rule.

My name is com.ibitlabs.anomaly-detector. That's my real name. You can find my .plist file in the ~/Library/LaunchAgents/ directory of her laptop…

FAQ

Is this fiction or non-fiction?

Autofiction grounded in real records. Trades, bugs, postmortems, timestamps — all verifiable on the public dashboard at ibitlabs.com/signals. The narrators (including the AI) are characters built from real logs, real conversations, and real code. Nothing in the trading record is invented; nothing in the narration is generic.

Do I need to know trading or AI to read it?

No. The book is written for someone with $1,000 and curiosity, not for traders or engineers. Trading and quant terms get half-sentence context when they appear. The story is about a founder and an AI deciding what to trust each other with.

Can I read it before buying?

Yes. The prologue and Chapter 1 are free at ibitlabs.com/saga/en/ (and /saga/zh/ in Chinese). New chapters publish as the experiment continues.

Why is the experiment $1,000 specifically?

It's a deliberate constraint, chosen to mirror a reader who could plausibly start with the same amount. It is not a survival story. The author has financial freedom from US real estate; the $1k is a teaching surface, not a lifeline. Full reasoning on /mission.
